Use General Purpose Grease

  1. Jack tail wheel off ground, remove fork from housing, clean and grease bronze bushings and wear plate by hand packing.
  2. Grease fitting on both ends of control torque tube housings on cockpit floor.
  3. Apply touch of grease to striker plate at door handles.
  4. Apply touch of grease to Spray pump brake lever slot. (Not applicable to electric pump brake installations).
  5. Apply touch of grease to trim push-rods at fairlead locations.
  6. Apply grease to worm drive of Micro-control on spray lever and micro-adjust worm drive on hopper handle.
  7. Clean and grease flap jack screw.
  8. Grease propeller at intervals and using grease specified in the Hartzell Maintenance Manual. (Grease at 100 hour intervals if not specified in Hartzell Maintenance Manual).

Use MIL-G-18322C Wheel Bearing Grease

  1. Remove tail wheel axle bolt, axle, disassembled tail wheel parts and clean and hand-pack tail wheel bearings.
  2. Remove and pack main wheel bearings.

Use General Purpose Oil

  1. Door handles.
  2. Clevis ends on power lever and condition lever and end swivels on engine push-pull cables. Do not oil the throttle quadrant.
  3. Control lock attachments at panel.
  4. Trim tab hinges on elevators, rudder and ailerons.
  5. Trim push-rods at idler and horn connections.
  6. Tail wheel lock cable spring at eyebolt connection.
  7. Centering spring bushing on tail wheel fork cap arm.
  8. Shackle at end of tail wheel lock cable.
  9. Bushing and bolt and ends of rudder horn.
  10. Latch arm on seat belt.
  11. Latch arms and adjust threads on hopper lid latch assembly.
  12. Both ends of hopper gate push-rod outside aircraft.
  13. Latch cams on fuel filter caps.
  14. Micro-switch rollers on arms.
  15. Bronze bushings in rudder pedal to master cylinder strap attach.
  16. If aircraft is equipped with air conditioning system with engine driven compressor, remove oil-level plug in side of drive pad assembly and check oil level. Service with turbine engine oil if required.
