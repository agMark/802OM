1. Always follow safety precautions.
  2. Do not discharge R134a into the atmosphere. Even though its ozone depletion potential is zero, it does have global warming potential. Recovery and recycling are mandated by the Clean Air Act. Use recovery equipment designated only for R134a. Never introduce another refrigerant into the R134a equipment.
  3. Never mix R134a with other refrigerants or air conditioning systems failure may to occur.
  4. Use only PAG lubricants for R134a systems using Sanden compressors. If other lubricants are used, air conditioning system failure is likely to occur.
  5. PAG oils used in R134a systems absorb atmospheric moisture very quickly. Moisture in the air conditioning system can cause major damage or failure.
  6. Never leave PAG oil exposed to air for a prolonged time. Tightly reseal the oil container immediately after each use.
  7. During air conditioning system repair, cap all fittings as soon as opened and leave capped until just before they are reconnected.
  8. If a repair is performed on an R134a compressor or system, evacuate the system for at least 30 minutes before recharging to ensure the removal of moisture which may have been absorbed by the PAG oil in the compressor and system.
