As a general rule if the Starter Generator functions normally in the Start mode there will be nothing wrong with it that would prevent it from functioning in the Generate mode.

Before attempting to correct any problem with this system first read and understand and acquaint yourself with the location and function of the various components. Look for obvious problems such as overheating, burned wires, loose components, and loose electrical connections. Disconnect the GCU Cannon plug and inspect it and all other electrical connections for corrosion. Correct any of these problems before proceeding.

Equipment

Equipment recommended for troubleshooting the charging system is shown below:

  • A multimeter capable of .01V resolution in the 0-2.5V range, .15V resolution in the 0-30V range and capable of reading from a fraction of an ohm to several kilohms will be required. An autoranging digital instrument is ideal but any good quality until will work. For point-to-point ringout, the ohmmeter function will be used.
  • GCU Breakout Box- This is not absolutely essential but it is very effective in localizing many problems. It can be fabricated per Fig Cross Reference below or it is available from your dealer.

Troubleshooting Procedures

Static Tests

Before going into specific symptoms, which will result in, operational squawks it is productive to point out a static test routine that is quick and easy and will result in localizing some problems without starting the engine.

Set Master Switch to OFF and Generator Switch to ON and check the following:

From To Normal Value Notes
GCU Pin L S/G Pin B 0 ohms If high, check Start Switch, Generator Switch, 15A C/B, and all associated wires and connections.
GCU Pin L S/G Pin E 0 ohms If high, check wire and connections from L to ground.
GCU Pin G S/G Pin E 0 ohms If high, check wire and connections.
GCU Pin M S/G Pin A 0 ohms If high, check wire and connections.
S/G Pin A S/G Pin E 2 ohms If over 5 ohms, suspect S/G field is open. If less than 1.5 ohms, suspect that S/G field is shorted for GCU M to S/G A wire shorted to ground.
GCU Pin D S/G Pin D 0 ohms If high, check wire and connections.
GCU Pin H LCR Pin X2 0 ohms If high, check wire and connections.
LCR Pin X2 S/G Pin E 56 ohms If over 75 ohms, suspect LCR Coil is open. If less, suspect LCR Coil is shorted or GCU H to LCR X2 Wire is shorted to ground.
GCU Pin A LCR Pin AZ 0 ohms If high, check wire and connections.
GCU Pin B LCR Pin A1 0 ohms If high, check wire and connections.

Scenario Troubleshooting - Generator Won't Come on Line

Evidenced by voltmeter reading below 25V and or low voltage light staying on.

Symptom Probable Cause Fix Notes
After engine start, GEN will not come on line unless the Start Switch is engaged momentarily after the Generator Switch is turned on. Field Flash function of GCU inoperative. Repair or replace GCU. The system can be operated as described without creating further detrimental effects until a convenient time to have the GCU replaced or repaired.
Symptom Probable Cause Fix Notes
During engine shutdown the starter continues to run if the Generator Switch is left ON. Reverse Current Protection function of GCU is defective. Repair or replace GCU. Turning the Generator Switch off prior to engine shutdown will correct this condition as a stopgap measure but the operator should be aware that if this precaution is overlooked damage to the starter generator can result.
Symptom Probable Cause Fix Notes
After an engine start the Generator will not come on line but normal generator output voltage exists (nominally 28V) at A1 of the LCR. This symptom would indicate that the Generator has built up and is being regulated but the LCR is not connecting the Generator to the Bus. This can be caused by:
  • Bad LCR, check for 56 ohms through LCR Coil.
  • Check the Main Bus Circuit Breaker.
  • No output (nominally 28V) on pin H of the GCU (defective GCU)
  • Output on pin H but no voltage on X2 of LCR (defective wiring).
  • Normal voltage (nominally 28V) from X2 to X1 of LCR (defective LCR).
  • Normal voltage (nominally 28V) from both X2 and X1 of LCR (open ground wire).

Repair or replace GCU, or

Ring out and repair wiring as required

Replace LCR
Symptom Probable Cause Fix Notes
After an engine start the Generator won't come on line and the voltage at A1 to the LCR is only about 1 Volt and there is no voltage on pin J of the GCU. There is a discontinuity in the circuit feeding power to the GCU, which includes the wiring, the 15A C/B, Generator Switch, and the Start Switch. Make sure that the 15A C/B is not tripped and that the Generator Switch is ON and then proceed to ring out and isolate the discontinuity from A1 of the LCR to pin J.
Symptom Probable Cause Fix Notes
The Generator won't come on line and the voltage on A1 of the LCR is low (9-16V). The Voltage Regulator section of the GCU is defective. Repair or replace the GCU.
Symptom Probable Cause Fix Notes
The voltmeter indicates rapidly increasing bus voltage and then at about 32V suddenly drops to about 24V and then slowly decreases as the batteries discharge. This is indicative of an Overvoltage trip and may be accompanied by the 15A C/B tripping. In those cases where the C/B does not trip the system can be restored to normal operation by momentarily turning the Generator Switch off and back on. If resetting the C/B or cycling the Generator Switch restores the system and the Generator stays on line the GCU is suspect of an intermittent condition. If the symptoms repeat the GCU is faulty and should be repaired or replaced.
