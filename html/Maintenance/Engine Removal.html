CAUTION

If an engine is being removed due to an unscheduled inspection, refer to the applicable Pratt & Whitney Engine Maintenance Manual. If the engine is being removed due to suspected or actual oil conamination, the airframe oil cooler and all asociated oil lines must be drained and flushed to prevent cross contamination of newly installed engine. The following components must be either repalced or sent to an appropriately rated faciclity for disassembly, inspection, flushing, and return to service.

  1. Propeller Governor (Primarily Governor)
  2. Overspeed Governor
  3. Fuel/Oil Heat Exchanger
  4. Propeller

NOTE

The propeller governor and fuel/oil heat exchanger will be furnished with the new engine. The overspeed governor is a airframe item.

CAUTION

If the engine being removed is expected to be inactive longer than seven (7) days, refer to the appropriate Pratt & Whitney Engine Maintenance Manual for preservation instructions. Ensure that the appropriate logbook entries asre made to document level of preservation and affix a tag or other identifying document to the engine stating that the engine has been preserved.

NOTE

Tag or otherwise identify all fuel and oil lines, hoses, electrical connectors, wires, nuts, bolts, and clamp locations during engine removal to ensure proper installation. Cap all lines and engine ports to prevent contamination.

NOTE

Ensure suitable containers are presesnt to capture drainted fuel and oil during engine removal.
