CAUTION

If an engine is being removed due to an unscheduled inspection, refer to the applicable Pratt & Whitney Engine Maintenance Manual. If the engine is being removed due to suspected or actual oil conamination, the airframe oil cooler and all asociated oil lines must be drained and flushed to prevent cross contamination of newly installed engine. The following components must be either replaced or sent to an appropriately rated facility for disassembly, inspection, flushing, and return to service:

  1. Propeller Governor (Primarily Governor)
  2. Overspeed Governor
  3. Fuel/Oil Heat Exchanger
  4. Propeller

NOTE

The propeller governor and fuel/oil heat exchanger will be furnished with the new engine. The overspeed governor is a airframe item.

CAUTION

If the engine being removed is expected to be inactive longer than seven (7) days, refer to the appropriate Pratt & Whitney Engine Maintenance Manual for preservation instructions. Ensure that the appropriate logbook entries are made to document level of preservation and affix a tag or other identifying document to the engine stating that the engine has been preserved.

NOTE

Tag or otherwise identify all fuel and oil lines, hoses, electrical connectors, wires, nuts, bolts, and clamp locations during engine removal to ensure proper installation. Cap all lines and engine ports to prevent contamination.

NOTE

Ensure suitable containers are present to capture drained fuel and oil during engine removal.

  1. Ensure battery switch (BATT) is OFF.
  2. Turn fuel valve selector lever to OFF.
  3. Remove engine side cowlings, top cowling, and bottom cowling.
  4. Disconnect batteries. (Accessible after removal of bottom cowling.)
  5. Remove exhaust stacks. Cover engine exhaust ducts to prevent FOD from entering engine.
  6. Disconnect the engine beta linkage and carbon block assembly from the beta ring.
  7. Remove propeller. (Refer to of this manual.)
  8. Disconnect nose landing light connectors and associated clamping hardware.
  9. Remove nose bowl.
  10. Remove cowling corner skins.
  11. Remove upper and lower engine intake plenum covers. Install FOD screen cover.
  12. Remove air filter and house assembly as follows:

    For aircraft equipped with Ram Air Induction System:

    • Remove air inlet tunnel.
    • Remove air filter.
    • Disconnect engine intake alternate air door control cable at clevis.

    For aircraft equipped with Filter Box Induction System:

    • Remove clamps and hoses connecting aft engine firewall to filter box. It is not necessary to remove the air filters for engine removal and replacement. Cover outlets on the filter box to prevent FOD from entering filters.
    • Remove all covers.
    • Remove screws from rear fire seal.
    • Remove struts.
    • Remove mid-bulkhead plate and attaching straps to forward bulkhead.
  13. Disconnect propeller control pushrod from primary governor.
  14. Disconnect Py line at primary governor, remove switch. (PT6A-65 or -67 only)
  15. Disconnect wires from oil cooler micro-switch and remove switch mounted on propeller control pushrod. (if installed) (PT6A-65 or -67 only)
  16. Disconnect overspeed governor cannon plug and associated ground wire.
  17. Disconnect torque pressure lines, and reference RGB static (Low) pressure line if equipped, from fitting(s) on engine reduction gear box (RGB). (Low torque pressure line on PT6A-65 or -67 only). (PT6A-67 has a restrictor fitting in the high pressure line).
  18. Remove engine RGB chip detector cannon plug.
  19. Disconnect propeller shaft drain line fitting at engine RGB (if equipped).
  20. Disconnect drain line from engine flow divider.
  21. Disconnect ITT harness from engine T5 terminal block.
  22. Disconnect (Np) tachometer generator cannon plug and associated ground.
  23. Remove engine bleed air line from engine compressor.
  24. Remove propeller control pushrod and control cable from engine, plenum aft bulkhead.
  25. Disconnect engine compressor wash line from plenum aft bulkhead.
  26. Disconnect engine intake wash line fitting from engine plenum rear bulkhead.
  27. Disconnect cockpit heater line at engine plenum aft bulkhead.
  28. Disconnect compressor drain line from fitting.
  29. Remove cover from plenum pressure switch mounted on aft bulkhead of engine intake plenum. Disconnect plenum pressure switch wiring.
  30. Disconnect engine ignition exciter cannon plug.
  31. Disconnect engine ignition exciter igniter leads.
  32. Remove engine intake plenum bulkheads.

    For aircraft equipped with Ram Air Induction System ,there are two bulkheads. The upper forward and aft bulkhead sections will be removed separately. The lower forward and aft bulkheads are attached to the filter cage and can be removed as an assembly. The alternate air door can remain installed on this lower assembly.

    For aircraft equipped with Filter Box Induction System, there are three bulkheads. All three engine bulkhead assemblies must be removed separately.

  33. Disconnect power control cable at FCU input lever.

    NOTE

    Retain input lever for installation on replacement engine.

  34. Disconnect condition lever control cable at FCU.
  35. Disconnect Py line at FCU. (PT6A-65 or -67 only).
  36. Disconnect fuel return/fuel pump vent line from FCU. (PT6A-60, -65, or -67).
  37. Remove beta light switch, bracket, and ground wires connected to engine inlet case flange. (Some applications have an alternate front mount beta light switch location).
  38. Disconnect fuel flow transducer cannon plug, (if equipped) and remove transducer.
  39. Disconnect oil temperature sender and remove sender.
  40. Disconnect oil pressure line from restrictor fitting in engine and remove fitting.

    41. NOTE

    Retain restrictor fitting for installation in replacement engine.

  41. Disconnect (Ng) tachometer generator cannon plug and AGB chip detector cannon plug (PT6A-65 or -67 only).
  42. Drain engine oil.
  43. Remove drain lines from engine accessory drive pads. (Starter generator, FCU, fuel pump, and air conditioning drive pad)
  44. Remove engine AGB breather line.
  45. Disconnect upper oil cooler oil line at engine oil tank fitting.
  46. Disconnect lower oil cooler line at engine AGB fitting.
  47. Disconnect main fuel line (see above) at intake side of engine driven fuel pump.
  48. Remove starter generator from QAD mount.

    NOTE

    If the starter generator is to be installed on the replacement engine, it may be convenient to leave the associated wiring connected and remove the S/G from the QAD mount, tying it back to support the weight.

    CAUTION

    The starter generator must be fully supported during the removal/installation. Do not allow the weight of the starter generator to rest on the spline drive as inserted into the engine or damage to the engine starter generator drive centrifugal breather carbon seal will result.

  49. Remove the air conditioner compressor assembly from the drive pad.

    CAUTION

    Do not disconnect air conditioning hoses or freon will be discharged. Tie compressor back to support the weight while engine change is in process.

  50. Attach the engine spreader bar to the engine hoisting lugs. Position a suitable hoist directly over the engine and attach the engine spreader bar.
  51. Raise the hoist as necessary to support the weight of the engine.
  52. Remove the engine mount hardware.

    CAUTION

    Prior to removing the engine from the airframe, ensure that all associated lines, hoses, wiring, cables, tubes, etc. are free and clear from obstructing the removal of the engine.

  53. Hoist engine clear of airframe and place in suitable engine stand or shipping container.
