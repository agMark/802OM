Several issues have been reported regarding the Air Tractor throttle quadrant when insufficient or no friction is applied. See Service Letters 341 and 352.

The proper setting of the friction adjustment knob is the tightest setting that still allows for smooth operation of the power lever. The knob should be tight enough so that there is no side-to-side play of any of the quadrant controls. Side-to-side play of the controls can lead to the power lever being able to move into the reverse position without actuating the reverse-lockout latch. This could possibly lead to a dangerous application of reverse-thrust in flight.

The friction adjustment knob should be tight enough that movement of the power lever will not affect the position of the condition lever (red) or propeller control lever (blue).

The knob should not be so tight that the power lever can't be operated without a rough or jerky motion.

The friction adjustment knob has right-handed threads so turning the knob clockwise tightens the knob and turning counter-clockwise loosens the knob.

If your throttle quadrant has not already been modified per SL352 to include the anti-reverse fence, this should be done as soon as possible.
