Contamination from foreign material can be found by looking at the oil drained from either the compressor or the suction and discharge lines. Contamination can also be seen collecting in the orifice tube or expansion valve, referenced below in Fig Cross Reference.

To illustrate how contamination will quickly destroy a good compressor, Fig Cross Reference shows a small metal shaving lodged under the discharge valve. The valve will open and close against the metal shaving resulting in a fatigue break of the valve. The broken valve is now free to travel inside the compressor causing additional internal failures. Eventually the compressor will stop pumping and over heat.
