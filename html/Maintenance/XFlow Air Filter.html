The 53474-1 filter assemblies are comprised of a cotton gauze element which is enclosed within stainless steel mesh. The 53474-1 filter assemblies are visually distinguishable from all other Air Tractor air filters by the blue molding.

To ensure proper function, the filter assembly must be cleaned and maintained regularly. The cotton gauze element is designed to efficiently separate particulates, dust and contaminates. The element must be properly oiled to ensure optimum performance.

The filter assembly must be cleaned and re-oiled at least every 12 months or sooner if:

  • If the AIR FILTER pressure differential switch is illuminated in flight or while the engine is operating.
  • If ingestion of FOD (birds, debris) is suspected.
  • If operating in an excessively dusty or dirty environment.

Regular cleaning of the air filter element will increase filter and engine performance.

Inspection:

Visually inspect for holes or ruptures in the filter element. Visually inspect for excessive dirt and debris and clean / re-oil as required.

Filter Cleaning and Oiling Procedure:

  1. Remove air filter from aircraft.
  2. Remove debris that can be easily separated from the filter element with a soft brush or vacuum.

    Caution

    Use of pressurized air may damage the filter element.

  3. Liberally spray or soak filter assembly (both sides) with Donaldson Air Filter Cleaner (Donaldson P/N 100205-000 or 100201-000). Allow to soak for approximately 30 minutes. After 30 minutes, rinse the filter with low pressure water. When the water running out of the filter is clear, the process is complete. Shake out as much residual water as possible without damaging the filter.

    Note

    Rinse opposite to airflow direction for best results.

    Note

    Use of a high pressure water nozzle may damage the filter element.

  4. Inspect the media for damage or holes by shining a bright light through the element. Inspect pleat structure, pleats shall be of regular pitch and shape. If required, pleats may be carefully re-formed with a hand seamer. After re-forming, inspect for mesh damage. If damage exists, discard filter assembly. Holes larger than 1/32 inch will allow excessively large particulates through to the engine so the filter should be repaired or discarded, see Section 5. If the filter element has holes larger than 1/2 inch, the filter element shall be discarded.
  5. Allow filter to dry.

    Caution

    Do not use forced hot air as it may damage the filter element or potting.

  6. Evenly apply 22 fl. oz. of Donaldson Air Filter Oil (Donaldson P/N 100105-000 or 100101-000) onto both sides of the filter element. Air Tractor packages and offers this oil as P/N SK1038-4. Allow at least 30 minutes for the oil to wick and migrate throughout the filter element. Inspect filter element for non-wetted areas. Locally apply up to 2 more fl. oz. Observe for any excess oil in the wire mesh or that is not entrapped in the cotton material, slowly wipe with a paper towel to remove excess.

    Note

    A low-pressure paint sprayer may be a convenient tool for applying the Air Filter Oil. If applying with a bottle and nozzle, the most efficient method is to apply a small stream on the peak of each pleat, flip the filter and repeat.

  7. Allow at least 30 minutes for the oil to wick and migrate throughout the filter element. Inspect filter element for non-wetted areas. Locally apply up to 2 more fl. oz. Observe for any excess oil in the wire mesh or that is not entrapped in the cotton material, slowly wipe with a paper towel to remove excess.

Repairs:

Holes or ruptures between 1/32 inch and 1/2 inch in the air filter element may be repaired and sealed with two part sealant (AMS-3276 or MIL-S-8802, examples: PPG PRC-DeSoto PR-1422 or PPG PRC-DeSoto P/S 890 “Pro-Seal”). Loose wires or loose sealant on the engine side of the filter pose a threat to the engine and are therefore unacceptable. If a hole or rupture penetrates entirely through the filter element, sealant must be applied to both sides. If a hole or rupture does not penetrate the engine side mesh, sealant need only be applied to the affected side.

When applied, sealant must completely fill the hole. Sealant must also be applied in the surrounding vicinity to ensure structural integrity approximately as shown below.

No more than 10 total ruptures may be repaired.

Limitations:

Owner's Manual supplement 04-0167 provided a single airworthiness limitation:

The Air Filter Assembly shall be replaced after no more than 15 cleaning and re-oiling cycles.
