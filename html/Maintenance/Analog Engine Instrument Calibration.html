Pressure Sensors

It is typical for operators to remove the torque, oil pressure and fuel pressure gauges from their aircraft in order to send them to a shop for calibration. This is a perfectly accpetable procedure.

If it is preferable to calibrate the gauges in the airplane, it can be accomplished using a pressure tester, also known as a dead weight tester, such as an Additel 927 or Barfield 2311F with an acceptable and calibrated gauge.

Disconnect the hose from the engine side, connect the tester and ensure that no air is in the lines then proceed with pressure testing.

CAUTION

Do not mix fuel and oil when calibrating.

Tachometers

The tachometers are the most difficult indicators to calibrate.

It is typical for operators to remove both the tach generators and indicators from the aircraft and send both for calibration. This is acceptable.

If it is desired to calibrate the tachometers without sending them for calibration, the most straight-forward method is to use a calibrated prop balancer and a signal generator / tachometer emulator. There are four basic tasks:

  • Verify NP tach generator calibration
  • Verify NP tach indicator calibration
  • Verify NG tach generator calibration
  • Verify NG tach indicator calibration

This procedure involves swapping tach generator locations for test. It may be performed in any order but it is preferable to perform the testing such that the components are not unnecessarily disturbed after testing is complete.

  1. Unplug cable from NG tach generator and install signal generator / tach emulator. Observe that the indicated NG % is the test NG % ± .2%. It is recommended that the emulator test speed is 100% NG but is not mandatory. [Verified NG tach indicator calibration]
  2. Unplug cable from NP tach generator and install signal generator / tach emulator. Observe that the indicated NP RPM is the test RPM ± .20. It is recommended that the emulator test speed is 1700 RPM but is not mandatory. [Verified NP tach indicator calibration]
  3. Swap NG and NP tach generator positions.
  4. Reinstall cables and run engine with prop balancing equipment installed. Operate engine through full prop speed range and verify NP indication is within 20 RPM of what is reported by the balancing equipment. [Verified NG tach generator calibration in NP position]
  5. Swap NG and NP tach generators to their original positions.
  6. Operate engine through full prop speed range and verify NP indication is within 20 RPM of what is reported by the balancing equipment. [Verified NP tach generator calibration]

ITT

This section provides instructions for checking the calibration of the ITT indicator for Air Tractor aircraft with Pratt & Whitney PT6A turbine engines. These instructions are written for use with a Barfield TT1200 Turbine Temperature Test Set. Other ITT calibration equipment may be used if that equipment is appropriate for use with Air Tractor ITT systems. When other equipment is used, the operator shall follow the manufacturer's operating instructions. Ensure that the test equipment has a current calibration before performing a calibration check of the engine instrument

The ITT system may be in one of two configurations: with lead resistance or without lead resistance. Calibration checking instructions for each style are different. Systems without lead resistance use an electronic indicator and are easily recognized by multiple pin connectors on the back of the instrument. These systems typically use a 2” instrument and are more common on newer aircraft. Systems with lead resistance have only two wires connecting the instrument to the engine. These systems typically use a 3” instrument and are more common on older aircraft.

Most aircraft manufactured after mid-2000 have an ITT instrument without lead resistance. These systems will not provide a temperature indication unless the indicator is powered with 24 to 28 volts, or normal aircraft power. The tester will be set up to simulate the ITT sensors in the engine. The output of the tester will be adjusted until the cockpit indicator shows a marked temperature (ex. 600°C). The calibrated temperature will then be read from the display of the tester. Use the format of the temperatures from the Sample Test Card provided in this section.

SYSTEMS WITHOUT LEAD RESISTANCE (Power Required)

  1. Disconnect aircraft power to the instrument. The simplest way to do this is to disengage, or pop out, the 5 amp "Engine Instruments" circuit breaker.
  2. Disconnect at least one of the instrument leads from the engine thermocouple terminal block. Note which lead was connected to which terminal.
  3. On the TT1200, set the “FUNCTION” switch to “IND TEST.”
  4. Set the “RANGE” switch to “20K=0Ω Sys Res.”
  5. Set the “1°C/.1°C” switch to “1°C.”
  6. Set the “°C/MV” switch to “°C.”
  7. Connect the TT1200 harness to the “TEST” connector on the TT1200 test set.
  8. Connect the black clip of the tester to the end of the instrument lead (Red) that was connected to the Alumel (-) terminal on the engine thermocouple block. Connect the red clip to the lead (Yellow) that was connected to the Chromel (+) terminal.
  9. Connect aircraft power to the instrument and turn the Master switch ON. Set the “ON/OFF” switch of the TT1200 to “ON”
  10. For the calibration check, the operator will need to take readings from the gauge inside of the cockpit as well as at the test set near the engine.
    1. Rotate the “TEMP ADJ” knob on the test set so that a temperature of 200°C is indicated on the ITT indicator in the cockpit. Record the temperature off of the TT1200 test set
    2. Repeat this for each temperature shown on the Sample Test Card.
    3. Adjust the temperature knob on the test set so that the ITT indicator lines up exactly with the red line. Record the temperature of the line, as well as the temperature indicated on the test set.
    4. Lastly, adjust the temperature knob on the test set so that the ITT indicator points at the red triangle. Record the temperature of the red triangle, as well as the temperature indicated on the test set.
  11. Once all temperatures are recorded from the TT1200 and the instrument, turn the airplane Master switch OFF, turn the TT1200 OFF, disconnect the test equipment, and return the aircraft to the configuration prior to testing.
  12. Compare the readings from the TT1200 and instrument. See the appropriate table below for tolerances. If the instrument is not within acceptable calibration, send the instrument to a shop for overhaul and calibration.

SYSTEMS WITHOUT LEAD RESISTANCE (Power Required)

  1. Aircraft power should be OFF during this procedure.
  2. Disconnect the leads from the back of the instrument in the cockpit. Note which lead connects to which terminal.
  3. On the TT1200, set the “FUNCTION” switch to “RES MEAS.”
  4. Set the “RANGE” switch to “200.”
  5. Attach the harness to the “TEST” connector on the TT1200.
  6. Set the “ON/OFF” switch of the TT1200 to “ON.”
  7. Short the tester by connecting the red and black clips of the harness together. While the tester is shorted, press and hold the “PTS” button while adjusting the “SYS RES” control knob until the “08.00Ω” is displayed on the tester.
  8. Disconnect the harness clips and set the “FUNCTION” switch to “IND TEST.”
  9. Set the “1°C/.1°C switch to “1°C.”
  10. Set the “C/MV” switch to “C.”
  11. Connect the black clip of the harness to the instrument terminal that had the Red (-) lead attached, and connect the red clip to the instrument terminal that had the Yellow (+) lead attached.
  12. For the calibration check, the operator will need to take readings from the gauge inside of the cockpit as well as at the test set near the engine.
    1. Rotate the “TEMP ADJ” knob on the test set so that a temperature of 200°C is indicated on the ITT indicator in the cockpit. Record the temperature off of the TT1200 test set.
    2. Repeat this for each temperature shown on the Sample Test Card.
    3. Adjust the temperature knob on the test set so that the ITT indicator lines up exactly with the red line. Record the temperature of the line, as well as the temperature indicated on the test set.
    4. Lastly, adjust the temperature knob on the test set so that the ITT indicator points at the red triangle. Record the temperature of the red triangle, as well as the temperature indicated on the test set.
  13. Once all temperatures are recorded from the TT1200 and the instrument, turn the TT1200 OFF, disconnect the test equipment, and return the aircraft to the configuration prior to testing
  14. Compare the readings from the TT1200 and the instrument. See the table below for tolerances. If the instrument is not within acceptable calibration send the instrument to a shop for overhaul and calibration.

Oil Temperature

Calibrating oil temperature requires the use of a heated oil bath. The temperature of the oil is to be monitored with an appropriate and calibrated thermometer. Mixing of the oil bath during calibration is important to avoid hot spots and erroneous calibrations.

Remove the probe from the engine and have an assistant monitor the gauge reading.

When calibrating the oil temperature gauge, the gauge should be accurate within the tolerances shown in the table below.

If the gauge is out of tolerance, it is useful to determine if the gauge or the probe is the source of the problem. The probe is an MS28034 temperature bulb which provides a resistance that varies with temperature as described in MIL-T-7990B. A high precision resistor can be used to simulate a temperature. Install the resistor across the cannon plug contacts. 100 ohms should result in a temperature gauge reading of 30°C.

Fuel Flow

Air Tractor does not recommend removing the fuel flow transducer from the fuel system for calibration. This is likely to cause problems when reinstalled.

Rather, fuel flow should be checked periodically by correlating the fuel consumed vs the amount added.

The acceptable tolerance is ±5% of the fuel consumed over at least 1 hour of flight.

Air Filter Indicator

The air filter indicator light is operated by a pressure switch located on the aft side of the engine intake plenum. To calibrate this indicator, remove the tube that enters into the plenum and attach a calibrated vacuum tester. If a water manometer is used in the calibration equipment, it does not require calibration.

When testing the indicator, the test point is to be observed when pressure is decreasing as there may be some amount of hysteresis in the switch and decreasing pressure simulates actual operation.

The switch should activate and illuminate the indicator at the following pressures:

18 ± .5 inches of water for -67AF/-67R/-67F engines

15 ± .5 inches of water for all other engines.

Beta Light

The beta light is a discrete signal from the beta and prop governor switches, there is no calibration required but it is advisable to observe that the indicator is functioning properly during ground operations.

Chip Detectors

Chip detectors cannot be readily calibrated. It is recommended that each is checked when the instruments are calibrated by shorting the sensing element with a piece of metal like a wire or paper clip.

Fuel Filter Indicator

The fuel filter indicator light is operated by a pressure switch located on the firewall. To calibrate this indicator, disconnect the hose attach a calibrated vacuum tester. If a water manometer is used in the calibration equipment, it does not require calibration.

The switch should activate and illuminate the indicator at 80 ± 2.0 inches of water.

Outside Air Temperature

The outside air temperature probe does not require calibration unless it is suspected of being inaccurate.

Volts / Amps

The volt / ammeter does not require calibration unless it is suspected of being inaccurate.

Ground Runs

Following completion of these calibration steps, an engine ground run should be performed. It is also an ideal time to to perform and log a performance ground run for trending purposes. Refer to the PWC manuals for procedures to perform this test.
