CAUTION

Prior to installing the engine in the airframe, ensure that all associated lines, hoses, wiring, cables, tubes, etc. are free and clear from obstructing the installation of the engine.

  1. Attach an engine spreader bar to the engine hoisting lugs. Position a suitable hoist directly over the engine and attach the engine spreader bar.
  2. Reverse engine removal procedures to install the replacement engine in the airframe.

    NOTE

    Prior to installing plenum covers, ensure engine inlet FOD screen cover is removed and inspect area for loose objects.

    CAUTION

    The starter generator must be fully supported during removal/installation. Do not allow the weight of the starter generator to rest on the spline drive as inserted into the engine or damage to the engine starter generator drive centrifugal breather carbon seal will result.

  3. Install air conditioner compressor assembly to drive pad. When installing pulley, align grooves with compressor belt grooves using a Dayco V-Belt tensiometer or equivalent. Adjust V-belt tension to 10 lbs. by turning the turnbuckle assembly.

    CAUTION

    If the drive belt is too loose, slipping may occur. If the belt is too tight, premature failures are possible in both the compressor and the drive pad bearings.

  4. Perform de-preservation of the FCU (if the unit being installed has been preserved) in accordance with the appropriate Pratt and Whitney Engine Maintenance Manual prior to engine startup.

    CAUTION

    On aircraft not equipped with FCU manual override controls, and FCU outfitted with override provisions, ensure FCU override lever is safety wired in the off position or damage to the engine will occur.

  5. Rig and verify operation of engine controls. See

    NOTE

    Specific checks and order in which they are to be accomplished after installation or replacement of engine and/or components can be found in the Powerplant-Adjustment/Test Section of the appropriate Pratt and Whitney Engine Maintenance Manual.

  6. Inspect and prepare for closeout.

    CAUTION

    Inspect entire area for security and routing of all wire harnesses, fuel lines, and oil lines. Inspect for loose objects or tools and ensure area is clean and clear of FOD prior to operational checks.

  7. Connect aircraft batteries.
  8. Turn fuel valve selector lever to ON.
  9. Perform functional tests/adjustments for Engine on Airframe replacement in accordance with appropriate Engine Maintenance Manual. Refer to the Airplane Flight Manual for limits and procedures.
  10. After completion of all leak checks, operational checks, adjustments, and tests reinstall remaining engine cowlings.
