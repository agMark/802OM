EM1 (100) Remove side cowling and examine exhaust pipe connections and check for cracks.
EM2 (100) Check security of fuel and oil lines and controls at all connections. Inspect for fretting/chafing of all lines and controls.
EM3 (100) Check controls on primary governor and controls on fuel control. Have someone in cockpit move start lever and then power lever through range of travel and look for loose connections, binding parts, rubbing, etc.

CAUTION

Do not move power lever into reverse position with engine stopped or controls will be damaged.
EM4 (300) nspect all cowling parts for cracks, especially at screw connections and in the corners of cut-outs.
EM5 (100) Inspect engine oil scavenge tubes and fuel lines at engine firewalls for signs of chafing. (Reference Service Letter #126).
EM6 (200) Inspect ITT wires and connectors.
EM7 (300) Inspect engine vibration insulators for cracks, swelling, or separation of elastomer.
EM8 (300) Examine engine mount for cracks (Reference Service Letter #253) and check torque at firewall connections. Torque is 4000 in-lbs on head of upper bolts and 1100-1300 in-lbs on nuts of lower bolts. Reinstall safety wire and cotter pins.
EM9 (12 Months) Remove and inspect P3 filter.
EM10 (2 Years) Verify calibration of ITT Indicating System. (See Maintenance Section, ITT INSTRUMENT CALIBRATION CHECK for procedures).
EM11 (2 Years) Verify calibration of Torque Indicating System
