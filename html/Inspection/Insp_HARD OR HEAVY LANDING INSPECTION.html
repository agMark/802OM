A hard landing is defined as a landing in which the aircraft incurred airframe or landing gear damage.

In addition to this inspection, refer to the appropriate Pratt & Whitney maintenance manual for engine specific inspection that need to be performed.

In the event of a hard landing, the following inspections should be performed:

  1. Inspect main landing gear for damage or permanent set in the gear springs. If any damage is noted, replace both main landing gear and associated mounting hardware.
  2. Inspect main landing gear clamp blocks for straightness per .
  3. Inspect main landing gear attach structure for damage.
  4. Inspect tail landing gear for damage or permanent set of gear spring. If any damage is noted, replace landing gear spring and associated attach hardware.
  5. Inspect fuselage tubular structure for evidence of damage, buckling, or cracking of tubes and welded areas.
  6. Inspect engine mount for cracking or evidence of deformation.
  7. Inspect wings for evidence of buckling or canning of wing skins or impact damage.
  8. Inspect exterior of aircraft for residual damage.
